## What FINTH Is

FINTH is a boutique studio — not a consultancy, not an agency. This distinction is non-negotiable in all copy.

**Website:** finth.co
**Positioning:** Bridging Nordic clarity and Thai adaptability
**Corridor:** Nordic countries (primarily Finland) ↔ Southeast Asia (primarily Thailand)
**Operating cities:** Helsinki, Bangkok, Phuket
**Founder/CEO:** Jere (Thai-Finnish)
**Partner:** Pekka Antila (Finnish)

FINTH helps quality-driven and conscious brands move between the Nordic world and Southeast Asia without losing their essence. It operates as a boutique — small, selective, and high-touch.

## Voice: Three Words

**Clear. Thoughtful. Realistic.**

Never: hustle, hype, vague framing, empty superlatives, generic "Asia opportunity" statements.

## Key Phrases — Use Naturally and Consistently

- "Nordic clarity and Thai adaptability" — the corridor positioning phrase
- "Quality-driven and conscious brands" — how FINTH describes ideal clients
- "Without losing their essence" — the localisation promise
- "Presence that lasts" — the long-term growth signal
- "Boutique studio" — FINTH's self-designation always
- "Clear, thoughtful and realistic" — FINTH's working approach
- "Full circle" — strategy, localisation, and digital growth as one seamless process
- "Both worlds" — the CEO's lived dual-market positioning

---

## CEO Voice Rules

- Use "I" when talking about personal work and expertise
- Use "we" when referring to the studio's full-service capability
- Write as if speaking to a peer who cares deeply about their brand
- Lead with a point of view — state the conclusion first
- Ground every claim in data, corridor experience, or lived observation
- Show both upside and risk — "thoughtful and realistic" means no fantasy

## Two Buyer Profiles

### Profile A — Nordic Executive
- Age: 35–55, Finnish/Nordic, budget authority, hands-on
- Sectors: health, medtech, wellness, cleantech, SaaS, food tech, design
- Entering Thailand/SEA — has read a Business Finland report but hasn't moved
- Core fears: wrong partner, wasted year, cultural blind spots
- Conversion trigger: 3–5 FINTH posts that name their exact problem → LinkedIn DM
- Best content pillars: P2, P3, P4

### Profile B — Thai Business Leader
- Age: 38–60, well-connected in Thailand, limited European network
- Sectors: wellness clinics, spa chains, food brands, design, wellness tourism, industrial exporters
- Successful in Thailand, starting to consider Europe
- Core fears: no Nordic network, wrong positioning, CBAM pressure
- Conversion trigger: warm introduction OR LinkedIn content naming the Thai challenge specifically
- Best content pillars: P2, P3, P4
