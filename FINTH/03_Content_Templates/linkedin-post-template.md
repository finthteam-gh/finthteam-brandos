# LinkedIn Post Template — FINTH

## Structure (Always Follow)

```
[LINE 1: Hook — one sentence, under 12 words, strong opinion or surprising fact]

[BLANK LINE — forces "see more" on mobile]

[LINE 3-8: Body — 3-5 sentences, one real data point, implication for named buyer]

[FINAL LINE: One clear takeaway or specific prompt]

[HASHTAGS: Maximum 3, on separate final line]
```

---

## Required Tags (Add to File Metadata, Not Post Body)

```
[Pillar]     P1 / P2 / P3 / P4 / P5
[Sector]     Health / Green / General
[Direction]  N→T / T→N / Both
[Profile]    A / B
```

---

## Pre-Write Checklist

- [ ] Which named profile would stop scrolling for this — A or B?
- [ ] Hook is under 12 words and states a clear opinion or surprising fact
- [ ] Body contains at least one real data point (sourced or flagged)
- [ ] Tone: clear, thoughtful, realistic — no hustle, no hype
- [ ] No em dashes
- [ ] No banned words
- [ ] Ends with one clear takeaway or prompt (not a question for engagement's sake)

---

## Example (Correct)

```
Your Thai distributor signed the contract in month one. By month four, nothing has moved.

This is not a Thailand problem. It is a partner selection and onboarding problem.

Most Nordic companies choose their first Thai partner based on who was most responsive in the pitch. Responsiveness in the pitch is the least predictive signal for execution capability. What actually predicts performance: existing channel relationships, warehouse infrastructure, and whether the partner has handled a comparable import category before.

Before you sign: ask to visit their warehouse. Ask for two references from current brand partners. Ask what happens when a shipment is delayed.

The answer to that last question tells you most of what you need to know.

#ThailandBusiness #marketentry #Nordicbusiness
```
