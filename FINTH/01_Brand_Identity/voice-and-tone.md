# Voice and Tone — FINTH

## Voice in Three Words
**Clear. Thoughtful. Realistic.**

---

## Do

- Write as if speaking to a peer who cares deeply about their brand
- Lead with a point of view — state the conclusion first
- Use first person: "I", "we", "FINTH"
- Name the specific buyer type: "a Finnish medtech founder entering Bangkok"
- Ground every claim in data, corridor experience, or lived observation
- Show both upside and risk — no fantasy

---

## Do Not

- Use: synergy, leverage, ecosystem, game-changer, disruptive, pivot, best practices
- Write: "Asia is a huge opportunity" — too vague
- Use "consultant", "agency", or "firm" to describe FINTH — always "boutique studio"
- Post motivational content disconnected from the corridor
- Claim expertise in: clinical health, carbon accounting, CBAM filing, ESG, legal advice
- Write for a vague global audience — always name the buyer type
- Use em dashes anywhere
- Use Title Case — sentence case always

---

## Key Phrases

| Phrase | When to use |
|--------|-------------|
| "Nordic clarity and Thai adaptability" | Core corridor positioning |
| "Quality-driven and conscious brands" | Describing ideal clients |
| "Without losing their essence" | The localisation promise |
| "Presence that lasts" | Long-term growth signal |
| "Boutique studio" | Always, when describing FINTH |
| "Clear, thoughtful and realistic" | FINTH's working approach |
| "Both worlds" | CEO's dual-market positioning |

---

## Voice Examples

**Correct:**
"Nordic brands carry something rare in Thailand: the trust of a country that takes quality seriously. Thai consumers know it. The question is whether your brand knows how to say it in Thai."

**Incorrect:**
"There are many great opportunities for Nordic brands to expand into the Southeast Asian market."

**Correct (market entry, specific):**
"Your Thai distributor signed the contract in month one. By month four, nothing has moved. This is not a Thailand problem — it is a partner selection and onboarding problem."

**Incorrect (vague advice):**
"Choosing the right local partner is very important when entering new markets in Southeast Asia."

---

## I vs We Rule

- "I" — personal work, expertise, CEO observations, lived experience
- "We" — studio capability, full-service delivery, team output
