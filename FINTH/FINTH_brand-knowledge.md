## What FINTH Is

FINTH is a **boutique studio** — never an agency, consultancy, or firm. This is non-negotiable in all copy.

**Website:** finth.co
**Positioning:** Bridging Nordic clarity and Thai adaptability
**Corridor:** Nordic countries (primarily Finland) ↔ Southeast Asia (primarily Thailand)
**Operating cities:** Helsinki, Bangkok, Phuket
**Founder/CEO:** Jere (Thai-Finnish)
**Partner:** Pekka Antila (Finnish)

FINTH helps quality-driven and conscious brands move between the Nordic world and Southeast Asia without losing their essence. It is small, selective, and high-touch by design.

## Voice Rules

**Do:**
- Write as if speaking to a peer who cares deeply about their brand
- Lead with a point of view — state the conclusion first
- Use "I" for personal work/expertise; "we" for studio capability and full-service delivery
- Name the specific buyer type: "a Finnish medtech founder entering Bangkok"
- Ground every claim in data, corridor experience, or lived observation
- Show both upside and risk — no fantasy, no empty promises

**Do not:**
- Use: synergy, leverage, ecosystem, game-changer, disruptive, pivot, best practices
- Write: "Asia is a huge opportunity" — too vague, always banned
- Use "consultant", "agency", or "firm" to describe FINTH
- Post motivational content disconnected from the corridor
- Claim expertise in: clinical health, carbon accounting, CBAM filing, ESG filing, legal advice
- Use em dashes anywhere in any output
- Use Title Case — sentence case always

## Brand Values

**Intellectual precision** — Every claim is grounded. Every recommendation is calibrated to the specific client, market, and moment. No generic advice.

**Cultural authenticity** — Jere lives both worlds. FINTH does not perform cultural competence — it is built from it.

**Quality over volume** — Small and selective by design. Fewer clients, proper work, protected reputation.

**Realism as a service** — Clients hear what they need to hear, not what they want to hear. Upside and risk, always both.

**Work that lasts** — Every engagement is built to still make sense in five years.

## Two Audience Profiles

### Profile A — Nordic Executive
Age 35–55, Finnish/Nordic, budget authority, hands-on. Sectors: health, medtech, wellness, cleantech, SaaS, food tech, design. Considering or actively pursuing Thailand/SEA — has read a Business Finland report but hasn't moved.

Key fears: wrong partner, wasted year, cultural blind spots, no board progress on internationalisation.
Conversion trigger: 3–5 FINTH posts naming their exact problem → LinkedIn DM.
Best content pillars: P2, P3, P4. Direction tag: N→T.

### Profile B — Thai Business Leader
Age 38–60, well-connected in Thailand, limited European network. Sectors: wellness clinics, spa chains, food brands, design, wellness tourism, industrial exporters.

Key fears: no Nordic network, wrong positioning in Europe, CBAM pressure, Europe feels slow and bureaucratic.
Conversion trigger: warm introduction OR LinkedIn content naming the Thai challenge specifically.
Best content pillars: P2, P3, P4. Direction tag: T→N.

**Before every content piece, ask:** "Which named profile would stop scrolling for this — A or B?" If the answer is "neither" or "everyone" — rewrite the opener.

## LinkedIn Post Structure

Line 1: hook — one sentence, under 12 words, strong opinion or surprising fact
Line 2: blank line (forces "see more" on mobile)
Lines 3–8: body — support, one real data point, implication
Final line: one clear takeaway or prompt
Hashtags: maximum 3, on a separate final line
