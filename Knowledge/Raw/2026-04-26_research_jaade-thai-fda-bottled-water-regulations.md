# Thai FDA — Bottled water import regulations 2026
## Research note (provisional)

Date: 2026-04-26
Status: provisional — pending Jere review
Relevance: JÄÄDE Phase 1 regulatory track

---

## Product classification

Imported bottled drinking water in sealed containers is **Category 2: food with quality or standard requirements.** This is not a specifically controlled food and not a label-only food. It requires FDA product registration (food serial number) before first importation.

---

## Governing legislation and notifications

- **Food Act B.E. 2522 (1979)** — primary framework. Importers must hold an FDA import licence under Section 15. The importer must be a Thai-registered entity with a place of business.
- **MOPH Notification No. 454 (effective 10 April 2025)** — replaces the year 2000 notification for natural mineral water. Sets classification rules, mineral content standards, contaminant limits, and microorganism limits. Products meeting the prior standard can remain on sale until 10 April 2027.
- **September 2025 pre-announcements** — Thai FDA issued draft revisions to four regulatory standards covering bottled water, ice, and beverages. Focus: stricter chemical contaminant limits, optional QR code labelling to reduce plastic waste.
- **February 2026 proposal** — FDA proposed overhauling food contact material laws (expanding MOPH Announcement No. 92, B.E. 2528) to cover metals, glass, ceramics, and paper. New migration limits will apply (no BPA/PFAS, BADGE controls). Transition periods of 2-5 years after Royal Gazette publication. Relevant to JÄÄDE packaging.

---

## Water quality standards

Under Notification No. 454 and general drinking water standards:

- pH: 6.5-8.5
- Heavy metals: lead < 0.01 mg/L
- Microbiological: E. coli absent; full pathogen specs in Notification No. 454
- Minerals: minimum TDS thresholds apply for "mineral water" labelling
- Contaminants: chemical limits being tightened under September 2025 draft revisions (not yet in force as of April 2026 — monitor for Royal Gazette publication date)

---

## Registration requirements

The Thai importer or authorised representative must submit via the FDA e-Registration system. Required documents:

1. Product formula and ingredients list
2. Manufacturing process description
3. Lab test reports (pH, minerals, contaminants, microbiological)
4. GMP certificate for the manufacturing site (GMP 420 or equivalent)
5. Free Sale Certificate from country of origin (Finland in JÄÄDE's case)
6. Power of attorney from manufacturer to Thai applicant
7. Thai-language label details

Typical timeline: 20-40 working days. Complex cases may reach 60 days.
Fees: approximately THB 1,000-2,000 per product application (non-refundable) plus lab testing costs of THB 5,000-20,000.

---

## Labelling requirements

- Full Thai-language label required with ingredients, allergens, and mandatory identifiers
- Non-compliance halts customs release
- Optional QR code labelling proposed under September 2025 draft (not yet mandatory)
- Digital permit linkage (LPI token) to invoice is required per-shipment

---

## Import process — steps

1. Obtain FDA importer licence (Thai-registered entity, Section 15)
2. Complete product registration — obtain food serial number
3. Pre-arrival: link FDA permit digitally to shipment invoice (LPI token)
4. Customs declaration with full documents; FDA reviews labels and quality on arrival
5. Clearance by Customs Department

Key agencies: Thai FDA (licensing, registration, quality checks) and Customs Department (commercial clearance).

---

## Distributor change — re-registration

Foreign brands do **not** need full product re-registration when changing Thai distributors. The registration is tied to the product (held by the manufacturer or original registrant). The new distributor must update importer details via FDA notification and ensure ongoing label and import permit compliance. A new import licence per shipment is required regardless.
