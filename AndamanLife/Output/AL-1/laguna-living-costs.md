# Laguna area living — what 50,000 baht monthly gets you

Laguna Phuket living costs run 30 to 40 per cent above equivalent properties elsewhere on the island. The integrated resort development across Bang Tao, Cherng Talay and the Laguna complex promises a European-standard lifestyle within a managed environment. For remote workers and early retirees with budgets starting at 50,000 baht monthly, the question becomes whether the premium delivers value in daily practice.

## Who chooses Laguna and why

European residents gravitate towards Laguna for three specific advantages: security consistency, amenity access, and proximity clustering. The development spans multiple villages but operates as a single managed zone with standardised services. Danish and German residents dominate the owner-occupier base, with British and Dutch nationals forming the largest renter groups.

Most residents work remotely for European companies or have retired early from corporate careers in technology, finance or consulting. The demographic skews 35 to 55 years old, with household budgets ranging from 50,000 to 150,000 baht monthly. They choose Laguna specifically to avoid the daily negotiations that define life elsewhere in Phuket.

Golf access drives many residency decisions. The Laguna Golf Phuket course sits at the development's centre, with most residential properties within walking distance or a short golf cart ride. Non-member green fees run 3,500 to 4,500 baht per round, while annual memberships start at 180,000 baht for weekday access.

## Housing costs across the integrated zones

Rental markets divide clearly between apartment complexes and standalone villa communities. Laguna area apartments typically range from 45,000 to 80,000 baht monthly for two to three-bedroom units. Villa rentals start at 60,000 baht for basic three-bedroom properties and reach 120,000 baht for premium four-bedroom houses with private pools.

Allamanda Laguna offers the most accessible entry point at 45,000 to 55,000 baht for two-bedroom apartments. Units include building pool access, fitness centre privileges, and 24-hour security. The development sits furthest from the beach but provides shuttle services to Laguna's beach clubs during high season.

Canal Village targets the middle market at 65,000 to 85,000 baht for three-bedroom townhouses. Properties include private gardens, communal pools, and golf cart access to the main resort facilities. Most units come partially furnished with basic kitchen appliances and air conditioning throughout.

Banyan Tree Residences commands premium pricing at 100,000 to 150,000 baht for four-bedroom villas. Rental terms typically include golf cart provision, daily housekeeping, and priority booking at resort restaurants. The location provides walking access to Banyan Tree Spa and direct beach access through the resort.

## Amenity access — what's included, what costs extra

Laguna's amenity structure operates on multiple tiers with varying access rights. Basic residential rental agreements include community pool access, fitness centre usage, and shuttle services within the development. Beach club access, golf privileges, and spa services require separate payment or membership arrangements.

Fitness facilities match European gym standards with modern equipment and air-conditioned environments. Most residential complexes maintain their own fitness centres, with larger facilities at Laguna Holiday Club available for 200 baht daily access or 2,500 baht monthly membership. Classes include yoga, pilates, and aqua aerobics taught by certified Thai and international instructors.

Pool access varies significantly across residential areas. Apartment complexes typically provide large communal pools with designated children's areas and poolside service. Villa developments often feature smaller community pools plus private villa pools for premium properties. Pool maintenance operates to international standards with regular chemical testing and cleaning schedules.

Security operates 24 hours across the development with controlled access points and regular patrols. Guards maintain English-language capability and coordinate with local police for any incidents. The system works effectively for peace of mind, though actual security incidents remain rare throughout Phuket's expat residential areas.

Internet connectivity meets remote work requirements with multiple redundant systems. TOT and AIS provide fibre connections delivering 100 to 500 Mbps download speeds consistently. Most residential properties include internet installation, though speeds may require upgrades for video conferencing demands. Backup 4G coverage through True and DTAC works reliably when primary connections face issues.

Power stability generally meets international standards with underground cable distribution reducing outage frequency. Most residential complexes include backup generators for common areas, though individual units may experience brief interruptions during severe weather. Installing a small UPS system resolves most connectivity issues for critical work periods.

Meeting space rental becomes complicated within Laguna's resort environment. Business centres at major hotels provide formal meeting rooms at 2,000 to 4,000 baht for half-day bookings. Restaurant private dining areas offer more flexible arrangements for client meetings, particularly during low-season periods when availability increases.

## Transport dependence and costs

Laguna's location requires transport planning for activities beyond the development. Taxis to Patong cost 400 to 500 baht each way, while airport transfers run 300 to 400 baht depending on timing and luggage requirements. No public transport serves the area effectively, making personal transport essential for regular off-site activities.

Motorbike rental provides the most practical solution at 3,500 to 4,500 baht monthly for automatic scooters. International driving licences work for short-term rentals, though Thai driving licences become necessary for longer-term insurance coverage. Most residential complexes provide secure motorbike parking with charging stations for electric models.

Car rental costs escalate quickly at 25,000 to 35,000 baht monthly for compact vehicles. Insurance requirements and parking availability within residential complexes require advance arrangement. The benefit comes from air-conditioned comfort and luggage capacity for airport trips or grocery shopping at larger outlets.

Shopping transport becomes particularly relevant given limited local options. Villa Market Laguna provides basic grocery needs at premium pricing, while major shopping requires trips to Central Floresta (20 minutes) or Central Festival (35 minutes). Monthly shopping trips to these centres typically cost 300 to 400 baht in taxi fees plus parking charges.

Laguna's European residential population numbers approximately 800 to 1,000 individuals during high season, dropping to 400 to 500 during low season months. The transient nature limits deep community development, though regular social groupings form around shared interests like golf, fitness, and dining.

The Laguna Business Network meets quarterly at different resort locations, focusing on professional connections rather than social activities. Attendance ranges from 25 to 50 participants depending on seasonal population levels.

Golf provides the strongest community anchor with regular tournaments, weekly games, and informal groupings based on handicap levels. The clubhouse serves as an unofficial community centre where residents connect over shared interests beyond golf. Language barriers remain minimal given the predominantly European membership base.

Family activities concentrate around resort facilities during school holidays and weekends. Children's clubs, swimming lessons, and family dining create natural connection points for parents. The international school bus route serves Laguna residents, though pickup times require coordination with work schedules for remote workers.

Restaurant and bar venues within Laguna host regular events including quiz nights, wine tastings, and live music performances. Prices reflect resort standards at 200 to 400 baht per drink and 800 to 1,200 baht per main course. The environment suits professional networking better than casual socialising due to pricing levels.

Medical facilities within Laguna include a clinic with basic services and English-speaking staff. Bangkok Hospital Phuket operates 25 minutes away for serious medical needs, emergency services, and specialist consultations. Health insurance requirements become essential given limited local medical capacity and high private healthcare costs throughout Thailand.
