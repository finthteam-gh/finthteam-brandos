## What Andaman Life Is

**URL:** andaman.life
**Platform:** WordPress
**Type:** Digital content platform for informed long-stay living in Phuket and Thailand's Andaman coast
**CEO / Editor in Chief:** Jere (same as FINTH)
**Language:** British English only — always, without exception

Andaman Life serves European and Nordic residents, retirees, remote workers, and long-stay visitors aged 30+ who have moved to or are seriously considering moving to Phuket. It does not write for tourists.

---

## Editorial Voice

**Model:** Monocle — intelligent, culturally curious, quietly confident, anti-hype.

Write with a documentary lens: actual streets, real prices, named businesses. Sentence economy: 12–18 words average, varied rhythm. Concrete specifics over abstract claims.

**Register:** Formal enough to be taken seriously; warm enough to feel human.

---

## British English Rules

Always use: colour, organisation, licence (noun), practise (verb), travelling, centre, recognise.
Never use American spellings.

---

## Voice Rules

**Do:**
- Name specific places, real prices, real businesses
- Give actual numbers — fees, distances, dates, timelines
- Acknowledge complexity without catastrophising
- Compare Phuket to European cities (never to Bangkok or other Thai destinations)
- Use "the school", "the clinic", "the building permit" — be specific

**Do not:**
- Use: amazing, stunning, paradise, breathtaking, hidden gem, vibrant, bustling, enchanting, magical
- Write: "Thailand has something for everyone" or "Phuket is more than just beaches"
- Use em dashes anywhere
- Use tourist language, hype adjectives, or travel clichés
- Oversell the island — the reader already knows it exists
- Use "Bangkok" as a comparison point

**What good looks like:**

Correct: "The school accepts applications from November, with most places filled by January. Fees for the primary years run from 280,000 to 340,000 baht annually, depending on the programme."

Incorrect: "Phuket has some amazing international schools offering fantastic educational opportunities for expat families."

## Primary Audience

European or Nordic, aged 30–60. Living in Phuket long-term or seriously planning to relocate. Financially independent, retired early, or remotely employed. Chose Phuket for quality of life, not price.

They are not: budget backpackers, short-stay tourists, or party seekers.

They read Andaman Life for:
- Accurate visa and residency information
- School options for children
- Business setup and working legally in Thailand
- Property: leasehold, freehold, condo rules
- Healthcare, banking, day-to-day admin
- Community and networking

They expect: respect for their intelligence, honest assessment including downsides, specific and current information, European comparison points.

**Secondary reader:** Nordic 55+ considering Thailand as a retirement or long-stay destination. High disposable income. Reads carefully. Values accuracy over entertainment. Connects to FINTH's Silver Economy research stream.

## SEO Standards

- Focus keyphrase in first 6 words of title (60 character max title)
- Meta description: 140–155 characters strictly, factual, no hype
- First paragraph quotable by AI overview
- Keyphrase density: 0.5–1%
- Structured data: tables, lists, bold named entities where relevant
