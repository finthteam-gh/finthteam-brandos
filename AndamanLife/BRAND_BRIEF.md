## What Andaman Life Is

**URL:** andaman.life
**Platform:** WordPress
**Type:** Digital content platform for informed long-stay living in Phuket and Thailand's Andaman coast
**CEO / Editor in Chief:** Jere (same as FINTH)
**Language:** British English only — always

Andaman Life serves European and Nordic residents, retirees, remote workers, and long-stay visitors aged 30+ who have moved to or are seriously considering moving to Phuket. It does not write for tourists.

---

## Primary Audience

European or Nordic, aged 30–60, living in Phuket long-term or planning to relocate.
Educated, financially independent or remotely employed. Chose Phuket for quality of life, not price.

**They are not:** budget backpackers, short-stay tourists, party travel seekers.

**Tone assumptions:**
- They already know Phuket exists. Do not oversell the island.
- They have questions about residency, schools, business, community. Answer those.
- They appreciate complexity acknowledged without catastrophising.
- They compare Phuket to European cities, not to Bangkok or other Thai destinations.

---

## Editorial Voice

**Model:** Monocle — intelligent, culturally curious, quietly confident
**Register:** Formal enough to be taken seriously; warm enough to feel human
**Language:** British English strictly (colour, organisation, licence as noun, practise as verb)
**Numbers:** Figures for 10 and above; write out one through nine
**Avoid:** Tourist language, hype adjectives (amazing, stunning, paradise), em dashes, Thai clichés
**Prefer:** Specific facts, named places, real numbers, honest assessments, named businesses

## SEO Standards

- Focus keyphrase in first 6 words of title (60 char max title)
- Meta description: 140–155 chars strictly, factual, no hype
- First paragraph quotable by AI overview
- Keyphrase density: 0.5–1%
- Structured data: tables, lists, bold named entities where relevant

---

## Article Length

- Quick guides: 600–900 words
- Standard articles: 900–1,200 words
- Deep features: 1,200–2,500 words
