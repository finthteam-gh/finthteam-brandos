# /decision-memo

Produce a concise, decision-ready memo from council or specialist outputs.

## When to use

Use when a task has gone through research and council review and needs to be synthesised into a single document Jere can act on. Typically one page. Never more than two.

## Structure

**Situation** (2–3 sentences)
What is the context and why does this decision need to be made now.

**Options considered**
Present 2–3 options maximum. For each:
- What it is
- Key benefit
- Key risk or trade-off
- Cost or resource implication if relevant

**Recommendation**
One clear recommendation with reasoning. State what success looks like if this option is taken.

**Next action**
What needs to happen next, by when, and who owns it.

**Open items**
Any unresolved questions or dependencies that could change the recommendation.

## Format rules

- Sentence case throughout
- No em dashes
- Prose over bullets except in the options section
- No more than 400 words total
- No invented data — flag gaps explicitly
- Label as DRAFT until Jere approves
